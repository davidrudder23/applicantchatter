ApplicantChatter is designed to help good companies hire good people.

ApplicantChatter is designed to help good people join good companies.

ApplicantChatter is the product of David Rudder. It is not officially associated with Door to Door Organics.

A Good Company

ApplicantChatter was founded by me, Door to Door Organics' employee David Rudder. I am the Manager of Engineering and IT. We have always found it a little tough to attract good software development, QA, IT and design talent because

  • We're in Colorado, which does not have the applicant pool of Silicon Valley
  • We're a small company without the brand recognition of VC-funded tech companies
  • We sell food, mostly produce. Not a typically hot segment for tech

But, Door to Door is a great place to work!

  • We're in Colorado, which is an awesome place to live
  • We're small. We have no politics. Our "work-life balance" actually exists.
  • We're in an incredible, nascent market. Food pervades our lives and we stand with a new wave of companies who are making food better. Better tasting, better for you, and better for the community.

But, every job on the market advertises how awesome a company they are. How do you prove that you actually are a great place to work?

"If they could only talk to us!"

Our job postings don't express our company culture. No block of text ever will. But, talking to us certainly does!

ApplicantChatter was inspired by a post on Hacker News. In it, a group of people were talking about an application to do basically the same thing, but it had tighter controls and no working code. The basic idea seemed very good. I knew I could build it easily if I made the rules less complicated.

The idea was to simply allow anonymous people to send emails to me. I would either answer them or farm them out to the team. I trust my team, and they trust me, and so extreme privacy/anonymity code was unnecessary. Besides, I could always wipe out the database, if necessary.

Some people will probably ask if the team is worried about repercussions from saying the wrong thing. The whole point of this is to address how difficult it is to hire good people. Why would I risk losing them over some stupid internet forum app? I wouldn't. We have a rule in our department; "don't be a jerk to each other". Everyone can assume we're all doing this for good reasons.

A Project Born

Door to Door Organics allows its employees to have side projects on personal time. Encourages it. Under the condition that it doesn't compete (which roughly translates into "don't do anything with food"). So, I didn't have to hide anything. I asked several people if they thought this would be useful. My VP, the Director of HR, my team, the Director of Marketing (who has a similar problem). Everyone seemed to think it was worth a try.

So, I did it. Enjoy.
